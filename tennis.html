Introduction

Tennis (or tennis in the literal translation) is a type of recreational sport in which two players compete in singles matches, or two teams of players compete in doubles matches. Each holds a racket to use to hit the ball over the net in the opponent's area. The number of offenses is not specified, but the score determines the winner. Racket or tennis is an Olympic sport that is played at all levels and groups of society and for all ages. Anyone can hold a racket and hit the ball, including people with disabilities and wheelchair users. Modern tennis originated in Birmingham, England in the late 19th century as a sport played on grass, so it is closely related to both field games such as cricket and bowling, as well as the ancient sport of racquet. Modern tennis rules have changed little since the 1890s, with two exceptions: From 1908 to 1961, a player was required to put one foot on the ground at all times of a match, and in the 1970s a tiebreak was introduced. One of the recent additions to the game at the professional level is the adoption of the electronic review technology (Eye of the Falcon) in addition to the points challenge system.

Tennis history

A game of ball and racket that has its origins in the eleventh century AD when the French lira arose, a stroke known as the tennis court, and then became popular in French royal circles during the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries, and was played with courtyard fences. The modern game of tennis originated in England in 1873.
